Optimization uses a best-fit-decreasing heuristic: pieces are placed largest-first into whichever open stock piece, or new stock option, uses the most material per dollar. It's fast and close to optimal in practice, but the general cutting-stock problem is NP-hard — for very large or unusual lists, a marginally better arrangement may exist. Kerf is charged between pieces sharing a stock piece, not after the last one.
